Best and Worst Bubble Tea Spots in Toronto

All Time Favorite: 1. Gong Cha

★★★★★

💬 QQ PEACH GREEN TEA, LIGHT ICE, 30% SUGAR. Enough said.

2. CoCo

★★★★☆

💬 COCO!!! The first time trying this chain I think was actually in China, before BBT became super popular in the West, so I'd consider myself an OG fan. This was my first time trying bbt so I'm obligated to place it in my top 3 but Coco never dissapoints. I'd consider it one of my safer options, I know that what I'm getting isn't going to be out of this world, but it's also never gonna be bad. One thing I will say, SWITCH BACK TO YOUR PLASTIC CUPS. I need to see what im drinking.

3. Chatime

★★★★☆

💬 For the last time, its CHAtime not CHAItime.

All Time Least Favorite: Preso Tea

★☆☆☆

💬 The memory of my first time trying Preso Tea has been burned into my brain like poisonous shards of hot metal. Around May 2023, I was in Markham with my parents, so I expected to get the HIGHEST quality of bubble tea, and I saw this little stand in the mall saying "Preso Tea". I'd never seen or heard of this bbt spot before, so I thought to give it a try. I get one of my go-to orders, I think it was the passionfruit green tea with tapioca or something and my god, was it a cup full of nothing. I always get 30% sugar but this drink tasted like straight coffee, and the pearls. They tasted like boiled cornstarch. Absolutely 0 taste. No words could describe the dissapointement I felt at this moment.

Second Worst: Real Fruit

★★☆☆☆

💬 Needs no introduction, when I see a plastic cup of yellow slush with a couple tapioca balls floating around, I know where this white person got their drink from. To be honest, there really isnt a valid reason I dislike this chain. I've had the mango slush drink whatever its called, and it isnt bad. The prices aren't insane, and the drinks aren't bad, its just... the vibe.

Third Worst: YiFang

★★★☆☆

💬 The tapioca pearls at YiFang may are towards teethless members of society, because there is no reason they need to be that soft. You could put them in my grandma's congee and she wouldn't notice anything different. The whole vibe and aesthetic of YiFang reminded me alot of Gong Cha so I wanted to give it a try, but safe to say they are not the same.
